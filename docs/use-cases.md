# Use cases — personas walking through the product

> _Five concrete journeys. Real workflows, real screens, real artifacts._

---

## Persona 1 — Maya, the working PM who wants to ship

**Context:** 33 years old. Senior PM at a SaaS company. Watching her engineering team ship AI features. Wants to do it herself — at minimum to evaluate roadmaps better, ideally to prototype on her own. Has 10–15 minutes between meetings, an hour at night when the kids sleep. Tried a Coursera course in January, abandoned by week 3.

**Day 1 — Onboarding (90 seconds)**
- Signs in with her Gmail.
- Picks `adult` / `Active builder` / interests `AI Foundations`, `AI PM`, `AI Builder mindset` / `10 min/day` / goal `Ship my first AI feature`.
- Maya's memory layer is seeded: *"Goal: ship my first AI feature. Wants 10 min/day. Background: PM."*

**Day 1 — First Spark (4 minutes)**
- Home shows: *"Today's quest — AI PM Level 1 — 'What an AI PM does differently'."*
- A 90-word MicroRead. A Quick Pick. A Tip & Trick *("Replace 'must always work' in your specs with 'must work in 95% of these 50 cases'.")*. A Pattern Match.
- Maya gets 36 ⚡. Streak: 1 🔥. Mascot says *"Nailed it!"*

**Day 7 — Build Card moment**
- Memory layer has noted: *strong on Quick Picks in AI PM, struggling on prompt-caching in AI Builder*.
- Home: *"You said you wanted to ship a RAG demo. Today's Build Card is a 20-line semantic search you can try in Claude Code right now."*
- Maya copies the prompt. Runs it. It works. She tells her team about it on Slack.

**Day 30**
- 28-day streak. Tier: Architect.
- Has shipped two real prototypes at work using LearnAI Build Cards.
- Her "Your Memory" tab shows 47 memories: goals, strengths (RAG basics, prompt engineering), gaps (cost engineering — fixing this week), preferences (vendor-neutral, prefers Anthropic).
- Posts a Tip she discovered to her builder profile (when social ships).

**Why she stays:** the system *knows her*. It never wastes her 10 minutes. Every Spark is one step toward the goal she explicitly stated.

## Persona 2 — Avi, the senior architect tracking the frontier

**Context:** 42. Staff engineer at a unicorn. Has built ML systems for a decade. Doesn't need basics. Needs distilled signal: what shipped this week, what's a real shift vs. hype, what tradeoffs the field has converged on.

**The thing he has today:** 12 Twitter tabs, 4 newsletters he never has time to read, a backlog of 80 saved papers.

**The thing LearnAI gives him:**
- Onboards as `Frontier visionary`. Skips L1–L4 in every Constellation he marks "I know this."
- Daily 5-minute "Pulse": 2–3 freshly-generated Sparks from the **AI Trends** + **AI News** Constellations, calibrated to his level. The cognition layer remembers the model versions he's worked with so it doesn't waste his time on basics.
- A weekly Boss Cell that tests him on the *new* stuff — last week's news, distilled into 3 sharp questions.
- His memory grows: *"Watched the o5 release. Skeptical of multi-agent papers. Likes pgvector."* The system stops recommending pgvector tutorials (he wrote one).

**The compounding payoff:** at year 1, his public profile shows *what he's mastered, what he's contributed, what he ships*. Recruiters approach him with offers that match his actual current skills, not a 5-year-old resume.

## Persona 3 — Sam, the kid (12 years old) who thinks AI is cool

**Context:** Heard friends talking about ChatGPT. Tried Khan Academy AI; bored. Watched 3 minutes of a 60-minute YouTube video.

**The age band shifts LearnAI's tone.** Onboarding picks `kid`. Sparks now use:
- Playful examples (cats, soccer, video games).
- Bigger illustrations, fewer code blocks.
- Quick Picks with 3 options instead of 4.
- A leaderboard of friendly bots Sam can beat.
- No Build Cards yet (re-enabled at age 14+).

**Day 1:** A 60-second MicroRead about *"What is an AI? — it's a brain that learns from examples, like you learning what a cat looks like."* Sam earns 8 ⚡. Mascot dances. Sam plays one more.

**Day 30:** Sam has earned the **First Spark**, **On Fire (3-day streak)**, and **Polymath** badges. He understands what tokens are, why models hallucinate, and how a recommender works. He shows it to his teacher. The teacher forks LearnAI for the school's after-school AI club.

## Persona 4 — Lin, the curious starter, age 58

**Context:** Recently retired marketing exec. Family asks her to "use AI" but she finds the tools intimidating. Wants to be conversant, not an engineer.

**Onboards as `adult` / `Curious starter` / `Stay current on AI news` / `5 min/day`.**

- Sparks lean toward **AI Foundations**, **AI News**, **Frontier Companies**.
- The mascot is friendly, never condescending.
- Build Cards are de-emphasized in favor of MicroReads and Tips.
- After 3 weeks, Lin can explain to her son the difference between a closed-API model and an open-weights one. She's proud. She tells two friends.

**The platform's lesson:** the same engine serves a 12-year-old, a senior engineer, and a retiree. The cognition layer carries the load.

## Persona 5 — Priya, the early-career researcher who wants to be visible

**Context:** PhD student, 26. Knows her subfield deeply. Wants to be discoverable by labs and startups. Frustrated with academic Twitter's signal-to-noise ratio.

**Onboards as `Frontier visionary` / `AI Builder` + `AI Trends` + `Open Source`.**

**She uses LearnAI in three ways:**
1. **Daily learning** — keeps her current outside her subfield (e.g. a vision researcher learning about agents).
2. **Contributing** (when this ships) — writes Sparks and Build Cards for her area of expertise. They go through AI-assisted review and a maintainer pass. Her name is on every Spark she authored.
3. **Public profile** — her builder profile shows: streak, tier, badges, contributed Sparks, topics mastered, recent Build Cards completed. Recruiters can search this.

**A year in,** Priya has authored 40 Sparks. She's the top contributor in AI safety. A Series-B startup messages her on the platform: *"We've seen what you've taught here. Want to chat about a research role?"* That's the **new LinkedIn moment.**

## Persona 6 — Daniel, the staff engineer who wants to hire

**Context:** Hiring manager at a mid-size AI startup. Needs three engineers who've shipped agentic systems with eval suites. LinkedIn search is full of resume keywords with no signal.

**On LearnAI** (Talent Match feature, post-MVP):
- Searches: *"Active in last 30 days · Mastered: AI Builder L8+, Memory & Safety L8+ · Authored ≥ 3 Build Cards in agentic stack · Tier ≥ Visionary"*.
- 47 candidates. He DMs 10. 6 reply. 4 take a call. 2 get hired in 3 weeks.

**The *why* this works:** unlike a resume, the LearnAI profile is *behavioral*. You see what they actually completed, what they taught, what streaks they kept. It's hard to fake daily activity over 6 months.

**The *why* this beats LinkedIn:** LinkedIn measures connections + claims. LearnAI measures *learned + shipped + taught*. Different signal entirely.

## Persona 7 — Anya, the educator who forks the engine

**Context:** Spanish teacher. Loves the Duolingo shape. Hates that Duolingo only does languages. Wants this for her domain.

**She forks LearnAI** (post-MVP open-source play):
- Renames it. Re-themes the brand in 5 minutes via the Admin → Branding tab.
- Replaces the 12 AI topics with 12 Spanish topics — JSON imported via Admin → Content → Import.
- Spins up her own mem0 instance (one Fly command).
- Invites her 30 students.

**Three months later:** Anya's fork has 800 weekly active learners across her school district. The engine maintained itself. She contributes a kid-friendly visualization back to mainline LearnAI. The platform becomes infrastructure for community-driven micro-learning.

## Cross-cutting moments worth designing for

Across all seven personas, the moments that *make* LearnAI are the same:

1. **The onboarding-to-first-spark moment** (~90 seconds). If they don't smile here, they don't come back.
2. **The "you know me" moment** (~Day 5–7). When the cognition layer surfaces an insight that's clearly *theirs*. *"I see I've been weak on safety — here's a sharp Spark"* > *"Today's quest: random topic"*.
3. **The "I built something" moment** (~Day 7–14). First Build Card that runs successfully. They feel like a builder for the first time.
4. **The streak moment** (~Day 7, Day 30). The badge unlocks. The streak number gets shareable. Identity shifts from *"learner"* to *"AI builder."*
5. **The teach moment** (~Month 2+). They contribute a Tip or correct an explanation. They're now part of the curriculum. (Future feature.)
6. **The hire moment** (~Month 6+). Someone reaches out about a role based on their public profile. (Future feature.)

Designing toward these six moments — not vague "engagement" — is the product's compass.
